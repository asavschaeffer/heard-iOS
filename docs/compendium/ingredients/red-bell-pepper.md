## Flavor Profile
Sweet and fruity with a mild vegetal note.

## History
Cultivated widely as a sweet counterpart to hotter peppers.

## Rules
Roasting deepens sweetness and adds smokiness.

## When to Break
Use raw for crunch even in long-cooked dishes.

## Pairings
Matches well with `compendium://ingredient.olive-oil` and
`compendium://ingredient.garlic`.

## Substitutions
For sweetness only, a small amount of tomato can work.

## Notes
Peel after roasting for a smoother texture.
